About the Project

Assistive feeding device that combines computer vision with robotics

Our assistive feeding device combines computer vision, robotics, and embedded systems to create an affordable solution for individuals with limited upper body mobility.

The system uses real-time mouth tracking to position food at the optimal location, giving users control over their feeding process. Built with accessibility in mind, the entire system costs under $500 and can be replicated by other teams.

Power Architecture

5V Rail (25W): Powers Raspberry Pi (3-7W), Intel RealSense camera (3-5W), and cooling fan (2W). Uses switching power adapter with ~85% efficiency.

9V Rail (27W): Dedicated servo power through BusLinker V2.2. Isolated from computing components to prevent motor noise interference.

Peak Consumption: ~50W with all servos under load during active feeding motion.

Signal Architecture

Camera Interface: USB 3.0 connection provides high-bandwidth RGB-D streaming at 30fps.

Servo Protocol: USB ‚Üí Serial Bus communication at 115200 baud. BusLinker converts USB commands to daisy-chained serial bus protocol for 4 servos.

Safety I/O: E-stop buttons wired directly to Raspberry Pi GPIO pins for <10ms emergency response time.

Safety Features

Dual E-Stops: Primary hard-wired button plus shoulder-mounted option for hands-free accessibility. Both trigger immediate motor power cut via GPIO monitoring.

Power Isolation: Physical power switch allows servo system shutdown while keeping Pi active for testing/calibration.

Thermal Management: Active cooling keeps Pi below 65¬∞C during continuous operation.
